World Where I Am Unwelcome: Complete Campaign Guide

Introduction

Welcome to World Where I Am Unwelcome, a dark fantasy tabletop RPG set in the tormented realm of Eyloria. You are the Antiheroes—outcasts chosen by fate to awaken the forsaken god Ksavir and shatter the tyrannical Light that rules with an iron fist. This guide is your key to the world: a comprehensive tome detailing the lore, history, geography, factions, plot, characters, monsters, rules, and advice for both players and the Game Master (GM). Within these pages, you’ll find everything needed to run a campaign of despair, vengeance, and redemption—or damnation. Prepare to journey through a land where hope is a fleeting whisper, and every choice could reshape reality itself.

The guide is divided into sections for ease of use: lore and worldbuilding, the campaign plot, detailed descriptions of locations, characters, and monsters, gameplay mechanics, and GM resources. Whether you’re a player crafting an Antihero or a GM weaving Eyloria’s dark tapestry, this book has all you need.

Lore and Worldbuilding

The History of Eyloria

The Age of Harmony (0-1000)

In the beginning, Eyloria was a realm of balance. Three gods held dominion: Ksavir, the god of forsaken souls, who offered refuge to the lost and broken; Elyria, goddess of purity, whose light cleansed the land; and Torval, god of order, who structured society with laws and faith. Mortals revered all three, building temples where the forsaken could find solace, the pure could shine, and the orderly could thrive. This was the Age of Harmony, a time of peace where Eyloria’s forests bloomed, its rivers ran clear, and its people lived without fear.

The Betrayal (Year 1001)

Harmony crumbled when Elyria and Torval grew jealous of Ksavir’s growing following. The forsaken—outcasts, sinners, and the broken—flocked to him, their numbers swelling. Fearing his power, Elyria and Torval conspired to betray him. They lured Ksavir to the southwestern edge of Eyloria, where they entombed his divine form in the Misty Forest, sealing him in eternal mist with a ritual powered by the nascent Mirror of Worlds. Ksavir’s screams echoed across the land, his blood seeping into the earth, birthing the Swamps of Despair. The Age of Shadows began.

The Age of Shadows (1001-Present)

With Ksavir gone, Elyria and Torval declared the Light supreme. They established the Light’s Faithful, a zealous order that hunted heretics and enforced their doctrine. Temples to Ksavir were razed, his followers branded as forsaken and driven underground. The land itself suffered—Morana’s dark magic corrupted the Cursed Forest, Zarkan’s chaos bled into the Mountains of Madness, and the Mirror of Worlds, now in Mortimer II’s hands, warped reality from Avalon. The Outcasts of Ksavir, a scattered resistance, whisper of his return, while the Light rules with an iron fist, their golden cities hiding a rotting core.

The Mirror of Worlds

The Mirror of Worlds is a divine artifact forged during Ksavir’s betrayal. Elyria and Torval imbued it with reality-warping power, using shards of celestial glass to bind Ksavir’s essence. Over centuries, the Mirror was shattered, its shards scattered across Eyloria—some held by mad guardians like Zarath, others buried in rifts like the Valley of Illusions. Mortimer II, ruler of Avalon, reassembled most of the shards, using the Mirror to bend reality: he can alter time, summon otherworldly beings, and create illusions that deceive even the gods. But the Mirror’s power is unstable, bleeding chaos into the world, creating rifts and corrupting all who wield it.

The Antiheroes’ ultimate goal is to destroy the Mirror, but doing so risks unleashing the chaotic energies it contains. The ritual to destroy it (Arcana DC 22) requires all shards to be gathered and a sacrifice of divine essence—potentially Ksavir himself, should he be awakened.

Geography of Eyloria

Eyloria is a land of stark contrasts, its beauty marred by corruption and oppression. It spans from the desolate southwest to the gleaming northeast, each region shaped by the gods’ conflict.

  • Southwest (Misty Forest, Alder Hamlet): A forsaken region where Ksavir’s influence lingers. The Misty Forest is shrouded in eternal mist, hiding his tomb, while Alder Hamlet sits on the graves of his army, its people under Torval’s watchful eye.
  • Central-West (Cursed Forest, Swamps of Despair): Lands scarred by divine sorrow and dark magic. The Cursed Forest, corrupted by Morana, is a twisted woodland, while the Swamps of Despair, born of Elyria’s tears and Ksavir’s blood, drown all hope.
  • Central-East (Lightfort): Elyria’s bastion, a fortress of light that hides zealous corruption. Its golden walls and radiant statues mask a fanatical populace.
  • Northeast (Valley of Illusions, Mountains of Madness, Creststone City, Avalon): The heart of the Light’s power, but also the epicenter of the Mirror’s chaos. The Valley of Illusions bleeds reality-warping magic, the Mountains of Madness hum with Zarkan’s chaos, Creststone City is Torval’s stronghold, and Avalon, the capital, is Mortimer II’s seat of power.

The Campaign: The Antiheroes’ Journey

Overview

You are the Antiheroes, outcasts marked by fate to awaken Ksavir and end the Light’s tyranny. Guided by Ysmera, Last Daughter, you’ll journey from the forsaken southwest to the gleaming northeast, gathering the Mirror’s shards, confronting the Light’s champions, and facing reality-warping horrors. Your ultimate goal is Avalon, where Mortimer II wields the Mirror of Worlds. Destroying the Mirror will free Eyloria—but at what cost? Will you restore balance with Ksavir, or plunge the world into chaos?

Journey Progression

Stage Location Objective Challenges Rewards
1 Misty Forest Meet Ysmera, learn of Ksavir’s tomb, and begin the journey. Survive Mist Wraiths, navigate the mist (Survival DC 15), interpret Ysmera’s prophecy (Insight DC 14). Knowledge of the journey, a minor relic of Ksavir (+1 to Wisdom saves for one PC).
2 Alder Hamlet Navigate Torval’s oppression and gather information. Deal with Father Gideon (Persuasion DC 16 or combat), fend off Grave Shadows at night (1d4+2). Information about the Cursed Forest, a silver dagger (deals radiant damage).
3 Cursed Forest Encounter Baba Yaga and uncover clues about the Mirror. Survive Corrupted Treants (2-3), negotiate with Baba Yaga or fight her (DC 16 Wisdom save to resist her curse). A map to the Swamps of Despair, a +1 dagger from Baba Yaga’s hut.
4 Swamps of Despair Face Kaelith and navigate the treacherous swamp. Defeat Kaelith (Level 10 Banshee), avoid Swamp Serpents (2d4), traverse difficult terrain (Survival DC 15). A vial of Ksavir’s blood (grants resistance to necrotic damage for 24 hours).
5 Lightfort Infiltrate Elyria’s bastion and confront Sister Lirien. Sneak past patrols (Stealth DC 20), defeat Sister Lirien (Level 10 Paladin), disable Zealot Constructs (2 per encounter). A radiant amulet (grants +2 to radiant damage rolls), intel on Creststone City.
6 Valley of Illusions Discern reality from illusion and deal with Thryme. Defeat Illusionary Phantoms (1d6 per wave), interpret Thryme’s visions (Investigation DC 18), resist illusions (Perception DC 18). A shard of the Mirror (grants +1 to Intelligence), clues about the Mountains of Madness.
7 Mountains of Madness Survive the chaotic environment and defeat Zarath. Resist madness (Wisdom DC 16/hour), defeat Zarath (Level 12 Sorcerer), fight Chaos Beasts (1d4+1). A major shard of the Mirror (grants +1 to a stat of choice), a chaotic staff (casts Chaos Bolt 3/day).
8 Creststone City Sneak through Torval’s stronghold and stop Veyra’s ritual. Avoid patrols (Stealth DC 20), defeat Veyra (Level 11 Bard/Necromancer), fight Necrotic Guardians (3 per encounter). A Cloak of Resistance (choose one saving throw, +1), intel on Avalon’s defenses.
9 Avalon Confront Mortimer II and destroy the Mirror of Worlds. Survive Mirror Sentinels (2d4), defeat Mortimer II (Level 15 Wizard), perform the ritual to destroy the Mirror (Arcana DC 22). Freedom of Eyloria, a divine boon from Ksavir (if awakened, +2 to a stat for all PCs).

Locations of Eyloria

Location Description Environmental Effects Notable Features GM Notes
Misty Forest The southwestern edge of Eyloria, where Ksavir’s body lies entombed in eternal mist. A dark, dense forest filled with whispers of the forsaken, its trees gnarled and black, its ground damp with sorrow. Thick mist (visibility 20 ft), whispers (disadvantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks, DC 14 Wisdom save to resist), cold air (Constitution DC 12 or 1 level of exhaustion after 4 hours). Ksavir’s tomb, a stone sarcophagus etched with runes, emanating a faint necrotic aura; hidden shrines to Ksavir, offering minor boons if found (Religion DC 15). Use eerie sound effects—whispers, distant wails, rustling leaves. The forest feels alive and hostile. Disturbing Ksavir’s tomb triggers Mist Wraith attacks. Ysmera appears here to guide the PCs.
Alder Hamlet A small village built on the graves of Ksavir’s army, its wooden homes decaying under Torval’s oppressive watch. The villagers are paranoid, fearing the undead that rise at night. Nighttime cold (Constitution DC 12 or 1 level of exhaustion), undead activity after dusk (1d4+2 Grave Shadows), oppressive atmosphere (Charisma (Persuasion) checks at disadvantage). The village square, dominated by a statue of Torval; a hidden crypt beneath the church, containing relics of Ksavir’s army (Investigation DC 16 to find). Create tension through social encounters—villagers may betray PCs to Father Gideon. Highlight the contrast between the quaint village and its dark underbelly: children whisper of “shadows in the graves.”
Cursed Forest A twisted woodland where Morana’s dark magic lingers, corrupting all life. Trees bleed black sap, animals are malformed, and the air is thick with a sickly sweet scent. Disorienting magic (Survival DC 16 to navigate), poisoned air (Constitution DC 14 or 1d6 poison damage/hour), corrupted ground (Dexterity DC 14 or slip, taking 1d4 piercing damage from thorns). Baba Yaga’s walking hut, a mobile structure on chicken legs (Perception DC 18 to track); pools of black sap that grant visions of Morana’s past (Arcana DC 15, but DC 14 Wisdom save or 2d6 psychic damage). Emphasize the unnatural—bleeding trees, warped animals, whispers of Morana’s name. The forest disorients PCs, making navigation a challenge. Baba Yaga’s presence should feel ominous and unpredictable.
Swamps of Despair A desolate marsh born of Elyria’s tears and Ksavir’s blood, where hope drowns in sorrow. The ground is muddy, the water murky, and the air heavy with despair. Difficult terrain (half movement speed), despair aura (Charisma DC 15 or disadvantage on Charisma checks), murky water (Perception DC 16 to spot hazards like quicksand, Dexterity DC 14 to avoid). Kaelith’s lair, a sunken shrine to Elyria now defiled by Ksavir’s blood; glowing will-o’-wisps that lead PCs astray (Survival DC 15 to resist). Use an oppressive atmosphere—constant drizzle, stench of decay, distant cries. Kaelith appears at dusk, targeting the most charismatic PC. The swamp should feel like it’s draining the PCs’ will.
Lightfort Elyria’s bastion, a fortress of light corrupted by zealotry. Its golden walls and radiant statues hide a fanatical populace, with public floggings in the courtyard. Bright light (disadvantage on Stealth checks), patrols (Stealth DC 20 to avoid detection), radiant aura (non-Light followers take 1d4 radiant damage/hour unless they succeed on a DC 14 Constitution save). The Hall of Purity, where Sister Lirien holds court, adorned with glowing crystals; a hidden armory with radiant weapons (Investigation DC 18 to find, guarded by Zealot Constructs). Highlight the contrast between beauty and oppression—gleaming walls, but screams echo from the dungeons. Lirien interrogates PCs; failure (Persuasion DC 18) leads to combat. Use Zealot Constructs to make infiltration tense.
Valley of Illusions A mystical rift bleeding from the Mirror’s power, where reality bends and mirages deceive. The air shimmers, the ground ripples, and voices echo from nowhere. Illusions (Perception DC 18 to discern reality), mental strain (2d6 psychic damage on failed check), shifting terrain (Dexterity DC 16 or fall prone, taking 1d6 bludgeoning damage). Thryme’s camp, a tent filled with mirrored surfaces that reflect distorted futures; a rift to the Mirror’s realm, leaking raw magic (Arcana DC 20 to harness, grants a 3rd-level spell slot but 1 Corruption Point). Use surreal descriptions—trees melting, mirrored PCs, voices of their past. Thryme’s reversed speech adds confusion. Phantoms exploit illusions, making combat disorienting.
Mountains of Madness Zarkan’s tomb, a chaotic range of jagged peaks where madness seeps from the earth. The air hums with chaotic energy, and the peaks shift unpredictably. Chaotic energy (Wisdom DC 16 or short-term madness/hour), unstable terrain (Dexterity DC 14 or 2d6 bludgeoning damage from falling rocks), random elemental storms (1/hour, DC 14 Dexterity save or 3d6 damage: fire, ice, lightning, acid). Zarkan’s tomb, a cavern pulsing with chaotic runes (Arcana DC 18 to decipher, reveals Mirror shard location); Zarath’s lair, filled with warped mirrors that reflect PCs’ fears (Wisdom DC 16 or frightened). Use chaotic effects—sudden storms, gravity shifts, whispers of Zarkan’s name. Zarath guards a Mirror shard, which PCs need. The environment should feel hostile and unpredictable, testing the PCs’ sanity.
Creststone City Torval’s stronghold, a grand city of light and oppression. Its streets are lined with statues of Torval, and the faithful rule with an iron fist, conducting public executions in the square. High alert (Stealth DC 20 to avoid patrols), crowded areas (disadvantage on Dexterity (Stealth) checks), oppressive chants (Charisma (Intimidation) checks at advantage, Persuasion at disadvantage). The Temple of Torval, where Veyra performs her rituals, surrounded by necrotic runes; a black market in the slums, selling forbidden relics (Persuasion DC 16 to access, guarded by Necrotic Guardians). Emphasize oppression—public executions, chanting crowds, the stench of burning flesh. Veyra’s ritual in the square is a focal point; interrupting it angers her. Necrotic Guardians patrol relentlessly.
Avalon The northeastern capital, seat of Mortimer II, where the Mirror of Worlds shapes reality. A gleaming metropolis with towering spires, but its heart is dark, warped by the Mirror’s power. Reality shifts (Investigation DC 20 to notice), random magical effects (DMG Wild Magic table, 1/hour), mirrored surfaces (Wisdom DC 18 or 2d6 psychic damage from seeing distorted futures). The Mirror Chamber, a crystalline dome in the central palace where Mortimer II resides; the Hall of Reflections, a gauntlet of Mirror Sentinels leading to the Chamber. Use epic descriptions—towering spires, air crackling with energy, reality bending (e.g., NPCs looping conversations). Mortimer II resides in the Mirror Chamber, guarded by Sentinels. Destroying the Mirror is the campaign’s climax.

Key Characters of Eyloria

Character Location Role Backstory Personality Stats Abilities Roleplaying Tips GM Notes
Ysmera, Last Daughter Misty Forest Guide of the Antiheroes Ysmera was Ksavir’s last priestess, killed during the Betrayal and bound to his tomb as a spectral guardian. She has waited a millennium for the Antiheroes, her soul tied to Ksavir’s will. Ethereal, mournful, and cryptic. She speaks in riddles, her voice a haunting whisper, and fears the Light’s wrath if she fails. Level 10 Ghost, AC 14, HP 80, Speed 0 ft, fly 40 ft (hover). Ethereal Sight (sees through illusions), Possession (DC 15 Wisdom save, one target), Prophetic Visions (reveals cryptic clues, Insight DC 14 to interpret), Draining Touch (+7 to hit, 3d6 necrotic). Her voice should echo, as if from a void. Use poetic language for her prophecies (e.g., “The forsaken weeps where light blinds”). She’s loyal to Ksavir, not the PCs—her guidance is genuine but vague. Ysmera appears when PCs enter the Misty Forest, offering a prophecy about their journey. She’ll guide them but flee if attacked. Use her to set the campaign’s tone—hopeful yet tragic.
Father Gideon Alder Hamlet Zealous priest of Torval Gideon was once a kind man, but decades of enforcing Torval’s will have hardened him. He lost his family to heretics, fueling his hatred for Ksavir’s followers. Stern, paranoid, and unyielding. He sees heresy everywhere, his voice booming with righteous fury, and he brooks no dissent. Level 8 Cleric, AC 16 (plate), HP 60, Speed 30 ft. Spells: Spiritual Weapon, Guiding Bolt, Hold Person (DC 15), Divine Sense (detects Ksavir’s influence), Mace (+6 to hit, 1d8+3 bludgeoning), Channel Divinity (Turn Undead, DC 15). His sermons are fiery—quote Torval’s scriptures (e.g., “The Light purges all shadows!”). He interrogates PCs with piercing eyes, quick to judge. Show his paranoia through nervous ticks (e.g., clutching his holy symbol). Gideon confronts PCs in the village square, demanding proof of their faith (Insight DC 16 to detect lies). If they fail (Persuasion DC 16), he attacks, summoning villagers as fodder (AC 10, HP 5). Use him to create early tension.
Baba Yaga Cursed Forest Ancient witch of Morana Baba Yaga is a millennia-old hag who served Morana, a demigod of decay allied with Ksavir. After Morana’s fall, she retreated to the Cursed Forest, weaving curses and bargains to survive. Cunning, cruel, and manipulative. She cackles with glee, her voice raspy, and loves twisting deals to her advantage. Level 12 Spellcaster (Hag), AC 15, HP 110, Speed 30 ft. Spells: Polymorph, Curse (DC 16 Wisdom save, -2 to all saves for 1 week), Counterspell, Magic Resistance, Walking Hut (moves 60 ft/round, AC 16, HP 80), Claws (+7 to hit, 2d6 slashing + 1d6 poison). Her laugh should unsettle—cackle mid-sentence. Offer deals with a sly grin (e.g., “Give me your happiest memory, and I’ll grant you power”). She’s unpredictable—play her as a wildcard who might help or hinder. Baba Yaga offers a deal: a map to the Swamps for a PC’s memory (removes 1 skill proficiency). If refused, she curses them and flees in her hut, summoning Corrupted Treants. Use her to test PC greed and morality.
Kaelith, Ashen Wraith Swamps of Despair Vengeful spirit Kaelith was a priestess of Elyria who fell in love with a follower of Ksavir. Betrayed by her order, she was drowned in the swamp, her spirit rising as a vengeful wraith, blaming the living for her fate. Sorrowful and wrathful, her voice a haunting wail. She’s consumed by vengeance, her eyes glowing with hatred. Level 10 Banshee, AC 14, HP 90, Speed 0 ft, fly 40 ft (hover). Wail (DC 15 Constitution save or drop to 0 HP, 60 ft radius), Incorporeal Movement, Draining Touch (+7 to hit, 3d6 necrotic), Frightening Gaze (DC 14 Wisdom save or frightened for 1 minute). Her wail should chill the air—describe the PCs’ breath fogging. She speaks in broken sobs (e.g., “You… let me… die…”). Target the most charismatic PC, reflecting her envy of the living. Kaelith appears at dusk in the swamp, her wail opening combat. She targets high-Charisma PCs, using Frightening Gaze to scatter the party. Her lair holds a vial of Ksavir’s blood—use her to show the swamp’s tragedy.
Sister Lirien, Blinded Saint Lightfort Devout follower of Elyria Lirien was blinded as a child by heretics, fueling her zeal for Elyria. She rose to lead Lightfort, purging darkness with divine light, her faith unshakeable despite the corruption around her. Fanatical, self-righteous, and resolute. She speaks with fervent conviction, her voice calm but commanding, believing her cause is just. Level 10 Paladin, AC 18 (plate + shield), HP 85, Speed 30 ft. Spells: Searing Smite, Zone of Truth (DC 16), Divine Smite (+3d8 radiant), Immune to fear, Blindsight 60 ft, Sacred Weapon (+7 to hit, 1d8+4 radiant, glows for 1 minute). Her blindness makes her other senses sharp—describe her tilting her head to “hear” darkness. She quotes Elyria (e.g., “Purity burns the wicked”). She’s unyielding—diplomacy is hard but possible. Lirien interrogates PCs in the Hall of Purity (Persuasion DC 18 to avoid combat). She fights with zeal, using Divine Smite on “heretics.” If defeated, she offers a radiant amulet as a plea for mercy. Use her to show the Light’s corruption.
Thryme, Backward Prophet Valley of Illusions Seer lost in time Thryme was a diviner who gazed into the Mirror of Worlds, its power shattering his mind. He now sees the past as the future, trapped in the Valley of Illusions, his prophecies a jumbled mess. Confused, erratic, and cryptic. He speaks in reversed sentences (e.g., “Future the see I”), his eyes wild with fear and wonder. Level 8 Diviner, AC 13 (robes), HP 50, Speed 30 ft. Spells: Mislead, Hallucinatory Terrain, Detect Thoughts (DC 15), Portent (2 dice/day), Staff (+5 to hit, 1d6+2 bludgeoning), Diviner’s Sight (sees 1 minute into the future 3/day). His reversed speech should frustrate but intrigue—pause mid-sentence, as if seeing visions. He’s not hostile unless attacked, but his spells (e.g., Mislead) make him slippery. Show his madness through disjointed actions. Thryme offers cryptic clues about the Mirror (Investigation DC 18 to decipher). He’s surrounded by illusions—PCs must discern reality to reach him. Use him to foreshadow the Mirror’s power and the chaos of Avalon.
Zarath, Shard Keeper Mountains of Madness Mad guardian of a Mirror shard Zarath was a sorcerer who sought the Mirror’s power, finding a shard in the Mountains of Madness. Its chaotic energy drove him insane, turning him into a paranoid guardian who hears the shard’s “whispers.” Mad, paranoid, and volatile. He rants incoherently (e.g., “The shard speaks! It burns!”), laughing and screaming in equal measure. Level 12 Sorcerer (Wild Magic), AC 15 (mage armor), HP 90, Speed 30 ft. Spells: Confusion, Fireball, Chaos Bolt (DC 16), Wild Magic Surge (roll on table each spell cast), Metamagic (Quicken, Twin), Chaos Staff (+7 to hit, 1d8+4 force). His madness should be vivid—laugh hysterically, then scream in terror. He casts spells erratically, often triggering Wild Magic (e.g., turning into a sheep mid-fight). He’s desperate to protect the shard. Zarath guards a major Mirror shard in Zarkan’s tomb—PCs must defeat him to claim it. His Wild Magic makes combat chaotic (e.g., accidental Polymorph). Use him to show the Mirror’s corrupting influence.
Veyra, Bone Singer Creststone City Leader of a death cult Veyra was a bard who turned to necromancy after Torval’s priests executed her family for heresy. She now serves the Light, using her songs to raise the dead and enforce Torval’s will, but secretly craves power. Cold, calculating, and charismatic. Her voice is hauntingly beautiful, but her eyes are dead, reflecting her inner emptiness. Level 11 Bard/Necromancer, AC 14, HP 80, Speed 30 ft. Spells: Animate Dead (summons 4 skeletons), Fear, Dissonant Whispers (DC 16), Song of Decay (+7 to hit, 3d6 necrotic), Bardic Inspiration (3d10/day), Necrotic Touch (+7 to hit, 2d8 necrotic). Her songs should unnerve—hum softly before casting Song of Decay. She’s manipulative, using charm to sway PCs (Persuasion DC 16 to resist). Show her inner conflict—she serves Torval but despises the Light. Veyra performs a ritual in the Temple of Torval—interrupting it angers her, summoning skeletons. She fights with necromantic magic, using Fear to scatter PCs. Use her to show the Light’s hypocrisy.
Mortimer II Avalon Ruler of Avalon Mortimer II inherited the Mirror of Worlds from his father, using it to cement his rule over Eyloria. He’s a master of reality-warping magic, but the Mirror’s power is slowly eroding his sanity, making him paranoid and ruthless. Arrogant, commanding, and disdainful. His voice is calm but authoritative, with a hint of madness—he believes he’s untouchable. Level 15 Wizard, AC 16 (shield spell), HP 120, Speed 30 ft. Spells: Time Stop, Mirage Arcane, Disintegrate (DC 18), Mirror of Worlds (warps reality: teleport 60 ft as bonus action, create illusions as a reaction), Arcane Ward (absorbs 30 damage), Staff of Power (+8 to hit, 1d8+5 force). He taunts PCs with cold confidence (e.g., “You think you can defy reality?”). His sanity slips mid-fight—describe his eyes twitching, his voice cracking. Use Time Stop to set up devastating combos (e.g., Disintegrate). Mortimer II is the final boss, residing in the Mirror Chamber. He’s guarded by Mirror Sentinels (2d4). Destroying the Mirror requires a ritual (Arcana DC 22)—he’ll fight to the death to protect it. Use him for an epic finale.

Monsters of Eyloria

Monster Location Lore Stats Abilities Tactics GM Notes
Mist Wraiths Misty Forest Ethereal beings born of Ksavir’s lingering essence, they haunt the forest, whispering curses to the living. They were once his followers, now trapped in eternal torment. AC 13, HP 45, Speed 0 ft, fly 60 ft (hover). Incorporeal Movement, Psychic Drain (+5 to hit, 3d6 psychic), Possession (DC 14 Wisdom save, one target), Resistance to non-magical damage. Ambush in groups (2-4), using the mist for cover (Stealth +5). Target low-Wisdom PCs with Possession, then drain others with Psychic Drain. Describe their whispers as maddening—PCs hear their fears (e.g., “You’ll fail…”). They attack if Ksavir’s tomb is disturbed. Use them to create a spooky opener, setting the campaign’s tone.
Grave Shadows Alder Hamlet Undead remnants of Ksavir’s army, buried beneath the hamlet. They rise at night, their forms flickering like shadows, seeking to reclaim their lost glory. AC 14, HP 50, Speed 40 ft, Stealth +5. Shadow Stealth (advantage on Stealth in dim light), Necrotic Slash (+6 to hit, 2d6 necrotic), Reforms unless killed with radiant damage, Vulnerability to radiant damage. Strike at night (1d4+2 per encounter), using Shadow Stealth to ambush. Focus on isolated PCs, aiming to overwhelm with numbers. Describe the ground cracking open as they rise, their eyes glowing faintly. Use them to make nights in the hamlet dangerous, forcing PCs to plan their rest. They’re a constant threat in this region.
Corrupted Treants Cursed Forest Once-noble trees twisted by Morana’s dark magic, their bark black and oozing, their branches like claws. They hate all living things, seeing them as threats to their corrupted domain. AC 16, HP 120, Speed 30 ft. Slam (+8 to hit, 3d6 bludgeoning), Entangle (DC 15 Strength save, restrained), Resistance to bludgeoning/piercing, Vulnerability to fire, Siege Monster (double damage to objects). Attack in groups (2-3), using Entangle to immobilize, then Slam. They target the nearest PC, ignoring strategy in their rage. Describe their twisted forms—black sap dripping, branches creaking like screams. They’re relentless—use them to make the forest feel alive and hostile. Fire is effective but risks spreading in the dry underbrush.
Swamp Serpents Swamps of Despair Venomous creatures born of the swamp’s corrupted waters, their scales slick with muck, their eyes glowing with malice. They hunt anything that moves, driven by primal hunger. AC 15, HP 40, Speed 30 ft, swim 40 ft. Bite (+6 to hit, 1d8 piercing + 2d6 poison, DC 14 Constitution save for half), Constrict (+6 to hit, 2d6 bludgeoning, DC 14 Strength save), Amphibious, Stealth +6 in water. Ambush in packs (2d4), hiding in water (Stealth +6). Bite first, then Constrict restrained targets, focusing on weaker PCs. Describe the water rippling before they strike, their hisses echoing. They’re opportunistic—target PCs who fall behind or enter water. Use them to make the swamp feel treacherous and alive.
Zealot Constructs Lightfort Animated statues imbued with Elyria’s light, their stone forms glowing with radiant energy. They were created to protect Lightfort, attacking heretics on sight. AC 17, HP 80, Speed 30 ft. Radiant Strike (+7 to hit, 2d8 radiant), Explosive Collapse (on death, DC 14 Dexterity save, 3d6 fire damage in 10 ft radius), Resistance to non-magical damage, Immune to poison/psychic. Patrol in pairs, attacking “heretics” (non-Light followers) on sight. Use Radiant Strike, then explode when destroyed, aiming to catch multiple PCs. Gameplay Rules

Character Creation

Basics

  • Level: Start at Level 5. The campaign is designed for 4-6 players, progressing to Level 10 by the end.
  • Classes: Use any standard D&D 5e class, reflavored to fit the dark fantasy theme (e.g., a cleric might worship Ksavir, a paladin might be a heretic hunter).
  • Races: Any D&D 5e race, but reflavor to fit Eyloria (e.g., elves as ancient forest dwellers, tieflings as descendants of Ksavir’s followers).
  • Ability Scores: Use standard array (15, 14, 13, 12, 10, 8) or point buy.
  • Backgrounds: Choose or create a background tying you to Eyloria’s conflict (e.g., a former Light zealot, a survivor of the Swamps of Despair).

Special Trait: Mark of the Forsaken

All PCs bear the Mark of the Forsaken, a faint scar-like rune on their skin, tying them to Ksavir’s cause. It grants:

  • Advantage: On saving throws against fear effects.
  • Disadvantage: On Charisma (Persuasion) checks with the Light’s Faithful, who sense the mark’s heresy.
  • Divine Connection: Once per long rest, a PC can commune with Ksavir’s essence (Religion DC 15), gaining a cryptic vision (GM discretion).

Core Mechanics (Based on D&D 5e)

  • Combat: Follow D&D 5e rules (attack rolls, AC, HP, saving throws, etc.). Use a grid or theater of the mind, as preferred.
  • Skill Checks: Use D&D 5e skills (e.g., Perception, Stealth, Arcana). DCs are provided for challenges (e.g., DC 15 Wisdom save to resist Possession).
  • Resting: Short rests take 1 hour, long rests take 8 hours. In dangerous areas (e.g., Cursed Forest, Mountains of Madness), long rests have a 50% chance of being interrupted by monsters (roll a d20: 1-10, encounter occurs).
  • Death Saves: Standard D&D 5e rules (3 successes to stabilize, 3 failures to die). If a PC dies, they can be revived by Ksavir’s essence (Religion DC 20, costs 1 Forsaken Point from each party member).

Special Rules

Forsaken Points (FP)

  • PCs start with 3 FP, representing Ksavir’s favor.
  • Spend 1 FP: Reroll a failed saving throw, skill check, or attack roll, or gain advantage on an attack roll.
  • Refresh: FP refresh after a long rest, or by performing a ritual to honor Ksavir (Religion DC 15, 1 hour).

Corruption

  • Exposure to the Mirror’s power (e.g., Valley of Illusions, Mountains of Madness) risks corruption.
  • Failing a Wisdom save against chaotic effects (e.g., DC 16 in the Mountains) grants 1 Corruption Point.
  • Effects: At 3 points, suffer disadvantage on Wisdom saves. At 5 points, suffer permanent madness (DMG madness table).
  • Removing Corruption: A ritual to Ksavir (Religion DC 18, 500 gp in materials) removes 1 point, or destroying a Mirror shard removes 1 point from all PCs.

Mirror Shards

  • Collecting a shard (e.g., from Zarath) grants a boon and a curse (GM discretion).
  • Example Boon: +1 to a stat for the party (e.g., +1 Intelligence after the Valley of Illusions).
  • Example Curse: Disadvantage on Wisdom saves for 1 week, as the shard’s chaos lingers.
  • All shards must be gathered to destroy the Mirror in Avalon (Arcana DC 22 ritual, requires a sacrifice of divine essence—e.g., Ksavir’s blood or a PC’s life force).

Advancement

  • Milestones: Level up after completing major objectives (e.g., surviving Misty Forest, defeating Sister Lirien, claiming a Mirror shard). Suggested progression: Level 6 after Cursed Forest, Level 7 after Lightfort, Level 8 after Mountains of Madness, Level 9 after Creststone City, Level 10 before Avalon.
  • Treasure: Find magic items in key locations (e.g., +1 dagger in Baba Yaga’s hut, Cloak of Resistance in Creststone City, chaotic staff in Mountains of Madness).
  • Experience (Optional): If using XP, award 500 XP per minor encounter (e.g., 2 Mist Wraiths), 1,000 XP per major encounter (e.g., Kaelith), and 2,000 XP per boss (e.g., Zarath).

Running the Campaign

Tone and Atmosphere

  • Maintain a dark, oppressive tone. Describe the decay of the land (e.g., “The trees weep black sap, their branches clawing at the sky”), the zealotry of the Light (e.g., “A child chants Torval’s name, her eyes hollow”), and the Mirror’s warping effects (e.g., “The air shimmers, your reflection showing a twisted future”).
  • Use sensory details—sounds (whispers in the Misty Forest, chants in Creststone City), smells (decay in the Swamps, incense in Lightfort), and visuals (shimmering illusions in the Valley, chaotic storms in the Mountains).
  • Make the world feel alive and hostile—NPCs are paranoid, the environment fights back, and the Mirror’s influence grows stronger as PCs approach Avalon.

Pacing

  • Each location should take 1-2 sessions (3-4 hours each). Allow time for exploration, roleplay, and combat, but keep the pace tight to maintain tension.
  • Use travel between locations for minor encounters (e.g., a lone Grave Shadow, a sudden storm in the Mountains of Madness) or environmental challenges (e.g., crossing a river, navigating a blizzard).
  • Build toward the finale in Avalon—escalate the stakes with each location. The Mirror’s effects should become more pronounced (e.g., illusions in the Valley, reality shifts in Avalon).

Player Choices

  • Diplomacy: Allow PCs to negotiate with NPCs like Baba Yaga or Sister Lirien, but ensure consequences (e.g., Baba Yaga’s curses, Lirien’s tests of faith). Use skill checks (e.g., Persuasion DC 16 to sway Gideon) to resolve non-combat solutions.
  • Stealth: Reward creative stealth in places like Lightfort and Creststone City (e.g., disguising as zealots, Stealth DC 20). Failure should lead to combat or capture (e.g., thrown into Lightfort’s dungeons, requiring a rescue).
  • Combat: Encourage tactical play—e.g., disbelieving Illusionary Phantoms (Investigation DC 16), using radiant damage against Grave Shadows. Punish recklessness with overwhelming odds (e.g., Zealot Constructs’ explosions).
  • Moral Dilemmas: Present tough choices—e.g., ally with Baba Yaga for power (at a cost), spare Sister Lirien (risking her return), or destroy the Mirror (potentially destabilizing Eyloria).

Foreshadowing

  • Drop hints about the Mirror early—Ysmera’s prophecies in the Misty Forest (e.g., “A mirror shatters the world”), Thryme’s visions in the Valley (e.g., “The glass king rules where light bends”).
  • Foreshadow Mortimer II’s power—NPCs in Creststone City whisper of “the king who shapes reality,” and reality shifts become more frequent as PCs near Avalon.
  • Hint at Ksavir’s potential awakening—his blood in the Swamps grants visions (e.g., “A shadowed god rises”), and relics in the Misty Forest pulse with his essence.
